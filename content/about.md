## About

I'm Thomas Huhn — entrepreneur, AI practitioner, and the author of The Convergence Thesis.

I don't write about AI from the sidelines. I build AI products. With [accessibleAI](https://accessibleai.eu), I develop software that helps companies automate regulatory compliance using large language models. Every day, I see firsthand how fast this technology is moving — and how most people still underestimate what's coming.

I also run [Rockstardevelopers](https://rockstardevelopers.de), a software agency, and [Consensus Ventures](https://consensus.ventures), my investment vehicle. This combination — building technology, running businesses, and investing capital — gives me a perspective that most commentators lack. I'm not just analyzing the machine economy. I'm building it.

### Why This Thesis

Most AI analysis falls into one of three camps: technicians discussing transformer architectures (too narrow), futurists philosophizing about AGI (too vague), or finance people recommending AI stocks (too shallow). 

I wanted to connect the dots that others view in isolation: **AI + Robotics + Crypto** — three pillars that are converging into a single investment framework for the machine economy. And I wanted to be honest about the implications — including the uncomfortable ones.

### The Newsletter

I write a regular newsletter analyzing developments at the intersection of AI, robotics, and crypto through the lens of the Convergence Thesis. New data, new developments, updated conviction — or updated skepticism.

No hype. No financial advice. Just an honest practitioner's perspective.
